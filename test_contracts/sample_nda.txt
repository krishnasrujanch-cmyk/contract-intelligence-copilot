MUTUAL NON-DISCLOSURE AGREEMENT

This Mutual Non-Disclosure Agreement ("Agreement") is entered into as of January 15, 2026
("Effective Date"), by and between:

Acme Technologies Pvt. Ltd., a company incorporated under the laws of India, having its
registered office at 42 Tech Park, Whitefield, Bengaluru, Karnataka 560066
("Disclosing Party / Party A")

AND

GlobalVentures Inc., a corporation incorporated under the laws of Delaware, United States,
having its principal place of business at 200 Park Avenue, New York, NY 10166
("Receiving Party / Party B")

ARTICLE 1. DEFINITIONS

1.1 Confidential Information. "Confidential Information" means any and all information or
data that has or could have commercial value, including technical data, trade secrets,
know-how, research, product plans, products, services, customers, software (including
source and object code), AI algorithms, model weights, training data, and evaluation
benchmarks disclosed by the Disclosing Party either directly or indirectly.

1.2 Exclusions. Confidential Information shall not include information that: (a) is or
becomes publicly available through no fault of the Receiving Party; (b) was rightfully
known to the Receiving Party prior to disclosure; (c) is rightfully obtained from a third
party without restriction; or (d) is independently developed without use of Confidential
Information.

ARTICLE 2. CONFIDENTIALITY OBLIGATIONS

2.1 Non-Disclosure. Each Party agrees to: (a) hold all Confidential Information of the
other Party in strict confidence; (b) not disclose to any third party without prior written
consent; (c) use solely for the Business Purpose; and (d) protect using the same degree
of care it uses for its own confidential information, but no less than reasonable care.

2.2 Permitted Disclosures. Each Party may disclose to employees, officers, directors,
contractors, and legal advisors who have a need to know and are bound by equivalent
confidentiality obligations.

2.3 Confidentiality Term. The confidentiality obligations shall remain in full force for
five (5) years from the Effective Date, regardless of whether this Agreement is terminated.

ARTICLE 3. INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY

3.1 Ownership. All Confidential Information shall remain the sole and exclusive property
of the Disclosing Party. Nothing herein grants any rights, by license or otherwise.

3.2 No License. This Agreement does not grant any right, title, or interest in any
intellectual property including patents, trademarks, copyrights, or trade secrets.

3.3 Work Product. Any joint work product shall be governed by a separate written agreement.

ARTICLE 4. TERM AND TERMINATION

4.1 Term. This Agreement commences on the Effective Date and continues for two (2) years.
Upon expiration, this Agreement shall automatically renew for successive one (1) year
periods unless either Party provides written notice of non-renewal at least sixty (60)
days prior to the expiration of the then-current term.

4.2 Termination for Convenience. Either Party may terminate upon thirty (30) days prior
written notice to the other Party.

4.3 Effect of Termination. Upon termination, each Party shall promptly return or destroy
all Confidential Information and certify such destruction within fifteen (15) days.

4.4 Survival. Confidentiality obligations survive termination for five (5) years.

ARTICLE 5. LIABILITY AND INDEMNIFICATION

5.1 Limitation of Liability. IN NO EVENT SHALL EITHER PARTY BE LIABLE FOR ANY INDIRECT,
INCIDENTAL, SPECIAL, CONSEQUENTIAL, PUNITIVE, OR EXEMPLARY DAMAGES, EVEN IF ADVISED OF
THE POSSIBILITY OF SUCH DAMAGES.

5.2 Direct Damages Cap. Each Party's total cumulative liability for any and all claims
shall not exceed United States Dollars Fifty Thousand (USD 50,000), regardless of the
form of action or theory of liability.

5.3 Indemnification. Each Party shall indemnify, defend, and hold harmless the other
Party from all losses, damages, liabilities, claims, and expenses including reasonable
attorneys fees arising from any breach of this Agreement.

ARTICLE 6. PAYMENT TERMS

6.1 Fees. Party B agrees to pay Party A a one-time access fee of United States Dollars
Twenty-Five Thousand (USD 25,000) within thirty (30) days of the Effective Date.

6.2 Late Payment. Unpaid amounts shall accrue interest at one and one-half percent
(1.5%) per month from the due date until paid.

ARTICLE 7. GOVERNING LAW AND DISPUTE RESOLUTION

7.1 Governing Law. This Agreement is governed by the laws of the State of Delaware,
without regard to conflict of law provisions.

7.2 Dispute Resolution. Disputes shall be settled by binding arbitration under AAA
Commercial Arbitration Rules in New York, New York.

ARTICLE 8. FORCE MAJEURE

8.1 Force Majeure Event. Neither Party shall be liable for failure or delay caused by
circumstances beyond reasonable control, including acts of God, pandemic, war, terrorism,
civil unrest, or governmental action.

8.2 Notice and Mitigation. The affected Party shall provide written notice within five
(5) business days and use commercially reasonable efforts to mitigate effects.

8.3 Extended Force Majeure. If the event continues for ninety (90) consecutive days,
either Party may terminate upon ten (10) days written notice.

ARTICLE 9. GENERAL PROVISIONS

9.1 Entire Agreement. This Agreement constitutes the entire agreement and supersedes
all prior agreements regarding the subject matter hereof.

9.2 Amendment. No amendment is valid unless made in writing and executed by both Parties.

9.3 Assignment. Neither Party may assign without prior written consent, not to be
unreasonably withheld. Assignment permitted in merger or acquisition.
